Phishing Awareness

Phishing is one of the most common types of cyber-attacks. Learn how to recognize phishing attempts and protect yourself:

  • What is Phishing? Phishing is a method of tricking individuals into divulging confidential information by masquerading as a trustworthy entity, typically via email or websites that appear legitimate.
  • Signs of a Phishing Email: Look out for emails that:
    • Contain grammatical errors or odd phrasing.
    • Ask for personal information like passwords or credit card numbers.
    • Contain suspicious links or attachments.
    • Claim urgency, such as "Immediate action required!" or "Your account is at risk!"
  • Don’t Click on Unknown Links: Always hover your cursor over a link to check its URL before clicking. If the URL seems suspicious or doesn’t match the source, do not click it.
  • Verify the Sender: If an email claims to be from a reputable source but seems off, verify the sender by contacting them directly using a known method (e.g., official phone number or website).
  • Use Anti-Phishing Tools: Many email clients and web browsers now include anti-phishing features that warn you about suspicious websites. Enable these features to add another layer of protection.
  • Report Suspicious Activity: If you suspect you’ve received a phishing email, report it to the relevant authorities or organizations, such as your email provider or the company being impersonated.

Why Phishing Awareness is Important:

Phishing scams are highly effective and can lead to identity theft, financial loss, and malware infections. By staying aware and cautious, you can prevent falling victim to these attacks.
